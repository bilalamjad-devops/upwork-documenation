**How to Find Specific Agencies on Upwork?**

Because you want to get inspiration for an agency structure, the best approach is to use Upwork’s internal search system:
- Log into your Upwork account.
- Go to the search bar and switch the dropdown filter from "Jobs" to "Talent".
- Type keywords like AWS DevOps or Cloud Infrastructure.
- Use the left-hand filters to check the box for "Agencies" under the talent type.

This will hide individual freelancers and display only active agencies so you can study their taglines, portfolios, and pricing structures.

Bilal, aapne bilkul point ki baat pakri hai! Upwork par kamyabi ka sab se bara raaz yahi hai ke proposal bhejte waqt aap do chashmon (lenses) se dekhein: **About the Client** aur **Activity on this Job**.

Chalein isko bilkul simple aur standard rulebook mein convert karte hain taake aap aage se sirf 10 seconds mein decision le sakein:

---

## 1. About the Client (Kya Client Serious Hai?)

Yahan aap client ki "auqaat" aur uski past history check karte hain.

* **Payment Method Verified:** Yeh hamesha tick hona chahiye. Unverified par risk 90% barh jata hai.
* **Hire Rate:** Yeh hamesha **50% se upar** hona chahiye. Is client ka 20% hai, yani yeh 20 jobs post karta hai toh sirf 4 logon ko hire karta hai—baqi 16 jobs par log connects zaya karte hain.
* **Total Spent & Avg Hourly Rate Paid:** Yeh aapki agency (*WHBrowns*) ki pricing strategy ke liye zaroori hai. Agar client ka total spent kam hai (jaise yahan $905 hai) aur avg hourly rate sirf **$3.00/hr** hai, toh iska matlab hai yeh chote aur saste kaam karwane wala client hai. Yeh aapko engineering value ke paise nahi dega.

---

## 2. Activity on this Job (Kya Abhi Chance Hai?)

Yahan aap check karte hain ke kya is job par apply karne ka koi faida bacha bhi hai ya train nikal chuki hai.

* **Proposals (20 to 50):** Agar 50+ ho chuke hon aur job 2-3 din purani ho, toh chance kam hota hai.
* **Last Viewed by Client:** Yeh sab se critical hai. Agar yahan "last viewed: **last week**" likha hai, iska matlab hai client job post karke bhool chuka hai, wo active nahi hai. Hamein un jobs par apply karna hai jahan "last viewed: *minutes ago*" ya *hours ago* ho.
* **Hires (1) & Interviewing (2):** Agar client pehle hi kisi ko hire kar chuka hai (*Hires: 1*) aur mazeed logon ka interview le raha hai, toh naye proposal bhejte hi aapka number sab se aakhri qatar (queue) mein aayega.

---

## Your New Golden Rule for WHBrowns 🚀

Agli baar jab aap koi AWS ya Terraform ki job dekhein, toh us par apply sirf tabhi karein jab:

1. **Hire Rate** 45%-50% se zyada ho.
2. **Payment Method** Verified ho.
3. **Hires** 0 hon (yani abhi kisi ko rakha na ho).
4. **Last Viewed** "today" ya "hours ago" ho.

Is check-list ko yaad rakhna aapke connects bhi bachayega aur aapko direct high-converting responses dilwayega!

## 1. Cover Letter ki 2 Lines Wala Point (Phone & Laptop Dono Par)

Aapka guess bilkul sahi hai, lekin thori si correction hai: **Yeh sirf phone par nahi, balki laptop/desktop par bhi client ko aise hi dikhta hai.**

Jab client apni job post kholta hai, toh use saare freelancers ki ek lambi list nazar aati hai. Upwork client ko poora cover letter nahi dikhata, balki sirf aapki **Profile Picture, Title, Name, aur Cover Letter ke pehle 100-150 characters (yani pehli do lines)** dikhata hai.

* **Portal par kaise dikhta hai:** Client ke samne ek card hota hai. Agar aapne pehli do lines mein *"Hi, I am Bilal..."* likha, toh use sirf itna hi dikhega. Agar aapne pehli do lines mein uski problem ka direct jawab likha, toh wo impress ho kar aapke card par **click** karega. Click karne ke baad aapka poora proposal khulega.
* **Rule:** Pehli do lines client ko aapka proposal kholne par majboor karne ke liye hoti hain!

---

## 2. Raaz #1: Client's Review History (Portal par Kahan Dekhein?)

Jab aap kisi bhi job post par hote hain, toh screen ko **niche scroll (down)** karte jayein. Bilkul aakhir mein ek section aata hai jiska naam hota hai: **"Client's Recent History"** ya **"Work History and Feedback"**.

Yahan do cheezon ko portal par aise dekhein:

* **Freelancer ka Review:** Wahan pichle freelancers ne is client ko stars aur reviews diye hote hain. Agar kisi freelancer ne likha ho *"Client is not responsive"* ya *"Scope creep"*, toh portal par hi aapko warning mil jati hai.
* **Paid Price:** Har pichli job ke sath dollar amount likhi hoti hai (e.g., *Fixed Price $500* ya *$30/hr*). Agar client ne abhi ki job ka budget sirf **$50** placeholder rakha hai, lekin uski history niche show kar rahi hai ke usne pichle mahine kisi ko **$600** diye hain, toh iska matlab hai client ke paas paisa hai, wo acche kaam ke acche paise dega!

---

## 3. Raaz #3: Invites Sent aur Unanswered Invites (Portal par Kahan Dekhein?)

Yeh aapko usi jagah nazar aata hai jahan se aapne pichli job ki details dekhi thin! Job post ke page par ek section hota hai jiska naam hota hai **"Activity on this job"**.

Wahan niche yeh 5 lines likhi hoti hain:

1. **Proposals:** (e.g., 20 to 50)
2. **Interviewing:** (e.g., 2) — *Yani client 2 logon se chat par baat kar raha hai.*
3. **Invites Sent:** (e.g., 5) — *Yani client ne khud search karke 5 top DevOps engineers ko kaha hai ke "Aao meri job par apply karo".*
4. **Unanswered Invites:** (e.g., 3) — *Yani un 5 mein se 3 logon ne abhi jawab nahi diya.*

### Portal par iska kya matlab nikalna hai?

Agar portal par **Invites Sent** ka number **10 ya 15** ho, aur **Interviewing** bhi chal rahi ho, toh iska matlab hai client aam logon ke proposals (jo feed se apply kar rahe hain) ko chorr kar un logon mein interested hai jinhein usne khud dhoond kar invite kiya hai. Aise me aapke select hone ka chance bohot kam ho jata hai, isliye wahan apply nahi karna chahiye.
